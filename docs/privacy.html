Longshot collects ZERO data. We believe privacy is a fundamental right, not a feature. Everything happens locally in your browser. We never see your screenshots, browsing activity, or personal information.

Overview

Longshot is a privacy-first Chrome extension designed to capture full-page screenshots of web pages. We built Longshot with a foundational commitment to user privacy, which means your data stays on your device where it belongs.

What Data We Collect

None. Longshot does not collect, store, track, or transmit any user data whatsoever.

Specifically, we do NOT:

  • Collect or store your screenshots
  • Track your browsing history
  • Send analytics or telemetry data
  • Upload anything to external servers
  • Require user accounts or authentication
  • Use cookies for tracking
  • Share data with third parties
  • Inject advertisements or tracking pixels

How Longshot Works

Understanding how Longshot operates will help you see why your privacy is protected:

  1. You trigger a capture: Click the extension icon or use a keyboard shortcut
  2. Local page analysis: Longshot reads the page dimensions in your browser
  3. Screenshot capture: Uses Chrome's built-in captureVisibleTab API to take screenshots
  4. Local stitching: Combines screenshots in an offscreen document in your browser
  5. Direct download: Saves the PNG file directly to your computer

Every step happens locally. No external servers are involved.

Permissions Explanation

Longshot requests several Chrome permissions to function. Here's exactly what each permission is used for:

activeTab

Allows Longshot to capture the visible portion of the current browser tab when you click the extension icon or use a keyboard shortcut.

tabs

Enables Longshot to read the page title and URL to generate descriptive filenames for your screenshots (e.g., PageTitle_hostname_timestamp.png).

downloads

Permits Longshot to save the captured screenshot PNG file directly to your computer's download folder.

scripting

Allows Longshot to inject a content script into web pages to detect scroll positions and measure page dimensions needed for full-page capture.

offscreen

Enables creation of an offscreen document where multiple viewport captures are stitched together into a single full-page image using OffscreenCanvas.

storage

Stores your extension preferences (like whether to expand content before capture) locally in your browser's storage. This data never leaves your device.

host_permissions (<all_urls>)

Allows you to capture screenshots on any website you choose to visit. Longshot only accesses pages when you explicitly trigger a capture and performs all processing locally.
